\section{1. Problem Statement \& Relevance}
{\small\itshape $\rightarrow$ Jury focus: Innovativeness (novelty, theme alignment) + Degree of Disruption (global relevance)}

\subsection{The Problem}
Every year, over 2.6 billion kg of returned products end up in landfills despite being perfectly usable. Returns cost Amazon \$25+ per item in reverse logistics, inspection, and restocking --- totaling billions annually. Meanwhile, customers distrust refurbished products, and sellers lack actionable insight into why items are returned.

\subsection{Why It Matters}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item 30\% of all online purchases are returned (vs 8\% in-store)
\item \$816 billion worth of merchandise was returned in the US alone in 2022
\item Only 5\% of returned items are resold at full value --- the rest are discarded or destroyed
\item Return shipping generates 24 million metric tons of CO\textsubscript{2} annually
\item Customers who buy refurbished report 40\% lower confidence than buying new
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Theme Alignment}
Our solution directly addresses ``Second Life Commerce'' by building an intelligent ecosystem where every returned product automatically finds its optimal next destination --- whether that's a nearby buyer, a refurbishment center, a donation partner, or a recycling facility. We don't just process returns; we prevent them before they happen and ensure every item that does come back gets a meaningful second life.

\subsection{What Makes This Novel}
No existing solution connects all three phases: \textbf{prevention} (before purchase), \textbf{intelligent resolution} (during return), and \textbf{circular recovery} (after return) into a single system. Our platform:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
\item Prevents returns before they happen through predictive purchase guidance
\item Resolves issues without physical returns when possible (saving logistics costs)
\item Matches returned items to nearby buyers in real-time (demand-aware routing)
\item Builds customer trust in refurbished products through certification + rewards
\item Makes sustainability profitable --- not just a cost center
\end{enumerate}

\section{2. Customer \& Solution}
{\small\itshape $\rightarrow$ Jury focus: Quality of Presentation (clarity) + Quality of Implementation (working prototype)}

\subsection{Target Customer}
\textbf{Three audiences, one connected platform:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{Customers}: Want to buy confidently, return easily, and trust renewed products
\item \textbf{Sellers}: Need to understand why products are returned and how to reduce returns
\item \textbf{Operations}: Need to process returns efficiently and maximize recovery value
\end{itemize}

\subsection{How We Solve It}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{Predictive Purchase Prevention} --- Before checkout, customers see guidance: ``This shoe runs small --- check the size guide.'' Reduces unnecessary returns at the source.
\item \textbf{Intelligent Resolution Center} --- Analyzes item value, shipping cost, and nearby demand to offer optimal resolution: keep-item refund, replacement, or standard return.
\item \textbf{Demand-Aware Circular Routing} --- Returned items matched to active buyers nearby and routed directly --- skipping warehouse entirely. Saves \$22+ per item.
\item \textbf{Certified Renewed Marketplace} --- Dedicated tab with quality grades, inspection transparency, and 90-day guarantee.
\item \textbf{Sustainability Rewards} --- Customers earn points for buying renewed, choosing keep-item resolutions, and sustainable actions.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{User Workflow}
\begin{verbatim}
CUSTOMER: Browse → Guidance → Buy → [Issue?] → Resolution Center
                                         ↓
          Instant Refund | Replace | Return → Drop off → Refund

AMAZON INTERNAL (invisible to customer):
  Return → Fraud Check → Quality Grade → Recovery Decision
                                              ↓
              Resell | Refurbish | Donate | Recycle
                                              ↓
              Demand Matching → Route to Nearest Buyer → Sold
\end{verbatim}

\subsection{Key Algorithms \& Complexity}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{Multi-Factor Recovery Optimizer (S6)} --- Weighted decision matrix evaluating 5+ recovery scenarios. Balances recovery value, carbon impact, processing time, fraud risk, and seller trust with penalty/bonus modifiers.
\item \textbf{Demand-Aware Circular Routing (S9)} --- Constrained optimization matching items to facilities by distance, capacity, type compatibility, and real-time demand signals.
\item \textbf{Predictive Return Prevention (S1)} --- Composite risk scoring using 9 weighted factors with actionable recommendation generation.
\item \textbf{Native Payload Compatibility} --- All 12 services designed for zero-transformation pipeline: S5 $\rightarrow$ S6 $\rightarrow$ S7 $\rightarrow$ S9 with direct output-to-input pass-through.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Scaling Strategy}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{Horizontal:} 12 services scale independently via ECS auto-scaling
\item \textbf{Event-driven:} EventBridge decouples all services. Adding service \#13 = zero changes to existing ones
\item \textbf{Graph intelligence:} Neptune handles fraud network queries in O(1) traversal vs 10+ SQL JOINs
\item \textbf{Global ready:} CDK deploys entire stack in new region in <10 minutes
\item \textbf{Cost:} Fargate = pay per task. At 1M returns/month: <\$2,000/month for all 12 services
\end{itemize}
